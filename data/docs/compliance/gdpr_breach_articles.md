# GDPR: Data Breach Notification Requirements (Articles 33 & 34)

## Article 33: Notification to the Supervisory Authority
- **Timeline**: Notification must occur **without undue delay** and, where feasible, not later than **72 hours** after becoming aware of the breach.
- **Threshold**: Required unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.
- **Content**:
    - Nature of the breach.
    - Categories and approximate numbers of data subjects/records.
    - Contact details of the DPO.
    - Likely consequences of the breach.
    - Measures taken to address/mitigate the breach.

## Article 34: Communication to the Data Subject
- **Timeline**: Without undue delay.
- **Threshold**: Required when the breach is likely to result in a **high risk** to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.
- **Exceptions**: Communication is not required if:
    - Technical/organizational protection (e.g., encryption) rendered the data unintelligible.
    - Subsequent measures ensured the high risk is no longer likely to materialize.
    - It would involve disproportionate effort (public communication is used instead).

## Summary for CISO
Proactive transparency is favored. Documentation of all breaches (even those not requiring notification) is mandatory for accountability.
